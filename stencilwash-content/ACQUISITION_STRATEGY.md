# Stencilwash Content Agency — Customer Acquisition Strategy

**Goal:** First paying customer within 30 days  
**Target:** $20K MRR by Month 6

---

## Target Customer Profile

### Primary ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Company Characteristics:**
- Developer tools, APIs, or open source projects
- Series A to C funding ($5M-$50M raised)
- 10-100 employees
- Technical founder or Head of Developer Relations
- Already producing some content but inconsistently

**Decision Makers:**
1. **Head of Developer Relations** (primary)
2. **VP of Marketing** (secondary)
3. **CTO/Technical Founder** (technical validation)

**Pain Points:**
- "Our blog hasn't been updated in 3 months"
- "Engineers don't want to write docs"
- "Our API docs are confusing users"
- "We need more technical content but can't hire full-time"
- "Our competitors outrank us for key terms"

### Secondary Segments

1. **API-First Companies**
   - Payment processors, infrastructure, dev tools
   - Need comprehensive docs + tutorials
   - Budget: $5K-$10K/month

2. **Open Source Projects**
   - Corporate-backed (e.g., CNCF projects)
   - Need ongoing tutorials and guides
   - Budget: $2.5K-$5K/month

3. **Technical Agencies/Consultancies**
   - White-label content services
   - Budget: Variable, project-based

## Acquisition Channels

### Channel 1: Direct Outreach (Primary)

**Strategy:** Targeted outbound to funded dev tool companies

**Tactics:**

1. **LinkedIn Outreach**
   - Build list of 100 target companies
   - Find DevRel leads via LinkedIn Sales Navigator
   - Personal connection request + value-first follow-up

   **Message Sequence:**
   ```
   Connection Request:
   "Hi [Name], saw [Company]'s recent post on [topic]. 
   Working on anything similar. Would love to connect!"
   
   Follow-up (if accepted):
   "Thanks for connecting! Noticed [specific observation about their content]. 
   We're helping dev tools like [similar company] with technical content. 
   Happy to share what's working—no pitch. Worth a brief chat?"
   ```

2. **Warm Introductions**
   - Leverage Eric's existing network
   - Ask for intros to DevRel leaders
   - Offer referral incentive: 1 free month for successful intro

3. **Twitter/X Engagement**
   - Follow target DevRel leads
   - Engage authentically on their posts
   - Build rapport before pitching

**Expected Results:**
- 100 connections → 20 conversations → 4 proposals → 1-2 customers
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks

### Channel 2: Content Marketing (Long-term)

**Strategy:** Demonstrate expertise through high-quality content

**Tactics:**

1. **Own the "Dev Content Agency" Niche**
   - Write about: How to hire technical writers
   - Metrics that matter for developer content
   - Comparing DevRel strategies

2. **Guest Posts**
   - Target: Dev.to, Hashnode, Hacker Noon
   - Topics: "What 50 DevTool Companies Get Wrong About Content"
   - Include soft pitch at end

3. **Case Studies**
   - First customer → detailed case study
   - ROI metrics: traffic, conversion, support ticket reduction

4. **Newsletter**
   - Weekly: "Best Dev Content This Week"
   - Builds audience of potential customers
   - Soft promotion of services

**Expected Results:**
- Month 1-2: Build authority
- Month 3-6: Inbound inquiries
- Long-term: Primary acquisition channel

### Channel 3: Community Presence

**Strategy:** Be helpful where target customers hang out

**Tactics:**

1. **Hacker News**
   - Comment on DevRel/content threads
   - "Show HN" post once we have first case study
   - Don't self-promote; add value first

2. **DevRel Discord/Slack Communities**
   - DevRel Collective
   - Write the Docs
   - Orbit Community
   - Participate, don't sell

3. **Conference Strategy**
   - Target: DevRelCon, LeadDev, Developer Marketing Summit
   - Month 3: Attend first conference
   - Month 6: Apply to speak

**Expected Results:**
- Network effects
- Referral source
- Brand building

### Channel 4: Strategic Partnerships

**Strategy:** Partner with complementary services

**Potential Partners:**
1. **DevRel Consultants** - They need execution arm
2. **Technical SEO Agencies** - Need content production
3. **Developer Marketing Agencies** - White-label content
4. **Open Source Maintainers** - Paid content sponsorships

**Partnership Structure:**
- 15-20% referral fee
- Co-branded case studies
- Mutual promotion

**Expected Results:**
- 2-3 qualified leads/month from partners

## Sales Process

### Stage 1: Discovery (15-min call)

**Objectives:**
- Understand current content situation
- Identify pain points
- Qualify budget/timeline

**Key Questions:**
1. "How often are you publishing technical content right now?"
2. "What's the biggest challenge with your current content?"
3. "Who's responsible for content currently?"
4. "What would success look like in 6 months?"
5. "What's your timeline for solving this?"

### Stage 2: Custom Proposal (24-48 hours)

**Deliverable:**
- Tailored content plan
- Sample topic ideas
- Timeline and deliverables
- Pricing

### Stage 3: Trial Article (Optional)

**Offer:** One paid trial article ($500)
- Reduces risk for prospect
- Demonstrates quality
- 70% convert to monthly retainer

### Stage 4: Close

**Closing tactics:**
- Start date flexibility
- Month-to-month option
- Case study references (after first customer)
